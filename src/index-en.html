About the project

Our goals


Resume

Feline Lower Urinary Tract Disease (LUTS) affects around 9% of domestic cats, and an average of 1.6 to 20% of these animals are diagnosed with Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD). These diseases can occur due to increased body weight or obesity, a sedentary lifestyle, excessive stress, an unbalanced diet and less water intake, the latter being the main reason for the onset of these diseases. As these animals usually need fresh, running water, the adoption of a drinking fountain is a better option. This work therefore proposes the development of an intelligent integrated system with an automatic drinking fountain and an informative mobile application, with the aim of reducing the chances of domestic animals showing symptoms of urinary tract diseases, especially felines, which are the subject of this research. The methodology was therefore divided into 7 stages: (1) a bibliographic survey on Feline Lower Urinary Tract Diseases (FLUTS) and Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD); (2) research into the technological hardware and software resources for developing the work; (3) definitions of the structure and models of adaptable drinking fountains; (4) definitions of the application and programming tools; (5) technical development of the project; (6) data collection, field research and interviews; and (7) testing and error correction. The expected results were the control of the water temperature, as well as the provision of information about the pet's water habits to the guardians, the connection between the sensors, the prototyping board and the drinking fountain used in the work, the full functioning of the application and its integration with the physical prototype, the dissemination of information to the population about the importance of the project and, finally, the reduction of risks of urinary diseases in felines.


Objective

The aim of this work is to achieve fundamental objectives for the development of the prototype and the research, which can be subdivided into two main axes: the general objective and the specific objectives. The general objective focuses on creating an integrated system for monitoring and controlling water consumption in cats, with a view to preventing urinary tract diseases. The specific objectives cover more detailed and technical aspects of the development. These include the implementation of an electronic circuit capable of capturing precise data on the water behavior of pets and the integration of this data with a mobile application that allows the guardian to monitor it in real time. Finally, it is hoped that there will be full interaction between the general and specific objectives pursued in the project, so that the development of the water monitoring system for cats can not only fulfill its practical function of preventing disease, but also demonstrate how technological solutions can be applied efficiently and affordably in animal care.


Justification

Feline Lower Urinary Tract Disease (FLUTD) and Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) affect a significant proportion of domestic cats, largely due to low water intake, sedentary lifestyles and inadequate diet. As cats tend to drink little water, especially if it is not fresh or on the move, the search for effective solutions to encourage water consumption is essential. This work is justified by the creation of an integrated smart drinking fountain and mobile app system, which aims to monitor water intake and prevent urinary diseases. The proposal contributes not only to the health of felines, but also to disseminating the importance of preventive care, using technology that is accessible and applicable to the daily lives of guardians.


Metodology

The methodology adopted for the project followed the engineering approach and was divided into seven stages: a literature survey on urinary tract diseases in cats; research into technological hardware and software resources; definitions of the structure and models of adaptable drinking fountains; definitions of the application and programming tools; technical development of the project; data collection, field research and interviews; and testing and error correction. The assembly was carried out in such a way as to guarantee the proper functioning of all the electronic components, allowing the sensors implemented in the physical prototype to capture precise data on the pet's water consumption and transmit it to the visualization environment.


Expected results

The system is therefore expected to function fully, accurately controlling water intake and temperature, as well as providing detailed data on pets' water habits to their owners. The integration between sensors, the prototyping board and the smart drinking fountain is essential to ensure the effective collection and transmission of this information. The project also aims to raise public awareness of the importance of water monitoring for cats, using the app as an effective channel for disseminating this information. Ultimately, it is hoped to reduce the risk of urinary diseases in cats, promoting a better quality of life for the animals and greater peace of mind for their owners.
